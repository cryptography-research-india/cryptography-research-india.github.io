Welcome to **Cryptography Researchers of India (CRIYPT)** — a community-driven platform connecting cryptography researchers from academia and industry, both within India and around the world.

## Our Mission

Cryptography is a field that underpins modern security, privacy, and trust in digital systems. India has a vibrant and growing community of cryptographers working on some of the most challenging open problems in the field — from theoretical foundations to real-world deployments.

CRIYPT was founded to:

- **Connect** researchers across geography and institution
- **Showcase** the breadth and depth of Indian cryptographic expertise
- **Facilitate** collaboration between academia and industry
- **Inspire** the next generation of cryptographers

## What We Cover

Our community spans the full spectrum of modern cryptography:

- **Symmetric Cryptography** — block ciphers, stream ciphers, hash functions
- **Public-Key Cryptography** — RSA, elliptic curves, lattice-based schemes
- **Secure Multiparty Computation (MPC)** — privacy-preserving protocols
- **Zero-Knowledge Proofs** — SNARKs, STARKs, and interactive proofs
- **Homomorphic Encryption (FHE)** — computation on encrypted data
- **Post-Quantum Cryptography** — lattices, codes, isogenies
- **Applied Cryptography** — blockchain, confidential computing, payment systems
- **Hardware Security** — side-channel attacks, secure enclaves

## How to Contribute {#contribute}

We welcome contributions from the community! Here is how you can help:

**Add a Researcher**
If you know a cryptography researcher of Indian origin who is not on our list, please submit a pull request to our [GitHub repository](https://github.com/cryptography-research-india/cryptography-research-india.github.io) or email us.

**Submit a Position**
Know of an open position in cryptography? Head over to our [Positions](/positions/) page to see how to submit it.

**Write a Post**
Have an announcement, opinion, or tutorial to share with the community? We welcome blog post contributions.

**Report Issues**
Found a mistake or a broken link? Open a [GitHub issue](https://github.com/cryptography-research-india/cryptography-research-india.github.io/issues).
